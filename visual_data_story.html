Car Speed by observing Road Type and Time

Road Type Records

A bar chart shows the number of car speed for Highway, Residential road and City street.

This first visualization shows that how many car speed records were collected for each road type, and it would help to compare the amount of data observing collections across different road types and help to show whether the data is balanced across the three road categories.

Average Speed by Road Type

A point plot shows the average car speed for each road type.

This second visualization shows that the comparison of average observed speed across different types of road, and it contains whether three road types tend to have higher or lower observing speed.For example, highways may be expected to have higher average speeds than residential roads because of different driving environment.

Speed by Time and Road Type

A box plot shows the distribution of car speed by record hour using timestamp data.

This third visualization shows that the Timestamp was converted into a date-time value using the lubridate package, and the record hour was used to compare speed pattern in different hours, including 12 and 13. This would help connect the speed observations with the time when data was recorded. It makes the visual data story more complete, because it not only compare road types, but also considers when the observations were made.
